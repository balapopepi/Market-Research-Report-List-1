<p><p>The crude salt market encompasses various types, each sourced differently. Sea salt is harvested through evaporating seawater, while well salt is obtained from underground brine wells. Lake salt is derived from natural saline lakes. Rock salt, mined directly from salt deposits, is often used industrially. Virgin salt refers to minimally processed salt, retaining trace minerals. Regenerated salt is produced from purification processes, often recycling waste salt. Each type has unique properties and uses, catering to diverse industrial and culinary applications.</p></p>
