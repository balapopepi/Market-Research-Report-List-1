<p><p>The compression coil spring market is segmented into various types based on their design and application. Conical springs taper at one end, allowing for compact storage. Variable pitch springs have varying distances between coils, offering distinct load characteristics. Barrel springs have a wider center and taper towards the ends, enhancing stability. Ordinary springs maintain uniform coil spacing, while "others" encompass specialized designs for specific applications. Each type meets unique operational requirements in industries such as automotive, aerospace, and manufacturing.</p></p>
