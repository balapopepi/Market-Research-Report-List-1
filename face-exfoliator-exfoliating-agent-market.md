<p><p>The face exfoliator market is segmented into physical and chemical exfoliating agents. Physical exfoliators utilize granular or textured materials to manually slough off dead skin cells, providing instant smoothness and improved texture. Common examples include scrubs and brushes. In contrast, chemical exfoliators use acids or enzymes to dissolve dead skin cells at a molecular level, offering a gentler and more uniform exfoliation. This category includes products containing alpha hydroxy acids (AHAs) and beta hydroxy acids (BHAs), which can enhance skin radiance and minimize pores.</p></p>
