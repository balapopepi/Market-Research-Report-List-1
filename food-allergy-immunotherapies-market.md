<p><ul><li>Subcutaneous Immunotherapy</li><li>Sublingual Immunotherapy</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The food allergy immunotherapy market consists of two main types: subcutaneous immunotherapy (SCIT) and sublingual immunotherapy (SLIT). SCIT involves administering allergens via injections, stimulating the immune system to build tolerance over time. This method typically requires a healthcare provider's supervision. In contrast, SLIT delivers allergens through tablets or drops placed under the tongue, allowing patients to self-administer at home, enhancing convenience and adherence. Both approaches aim to reduce allergic reactions and improve quality of life for individuals with food allergies.</p></p>
