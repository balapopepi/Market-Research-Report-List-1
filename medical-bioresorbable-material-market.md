<p><p>The medical bioresorbable materials market includes various types such as PLA (polylactic acid), PGA (polyglycolic acid), and PCL (polycaprolactone), each serving unique applications in biomedical devices. PLA is favored for its strength and biodegradability, while PGA is known for its rapid absorption in the body. PCL, with a slower degradation rate, is ideal for long-term applications. Additionally, polysaccharides, derived from natural sources, offer biocompatibility, and "others" may encompass innovative materials, expanding options for medical applications.</p></p>
