<p><p>One-part epoxy resin adhesives are versatile materials that cure through different processes. Heat-curing epoxy requires elevated temperatures to initiate the hardening process, resulting in strong bonds suitable for high-performance applications like aerospace and automotive. In contrast, ultraviolet (UV) curing epoxy hardens when exposed to UV light, allowing for rapid adhesion and minimal setup time, making it ideal for applications in packaging and electronics. Both types serve distinct market needs based on their curing methods and application requirements.</p></p>
