<p><p>The cable modem market is primarily divided into two types: wired and wireless cable modems. Wired cable modems connect directly to the cable service provider via a coaxial cable, offering stable and high-speed internet access. In contrast, wireless cable modems integrate Wi-Fi capabilities, allowing multiple devices to connect to the internet without cables. While wired modems excel in speed and reliability, wireless models provide convenience and flexibility, catering to varying user preferences and needs in broadband connectivity.</p></p>
